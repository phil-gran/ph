" Working with objections and questions."



Objection: the program is too expensive.


 What you say: Yes, the program is not cheap. But it is significantly less expensive than a traditional college education. Plus, you do not have to pay anything until and unless you have a job. And since it is in our interest for you to get a job quickly, we have a whole Job Support department dedicated to helping graduates land their dream job. 95% of graduates land a job within a few weeks of finishing. And the starting salary of a web developer is around $70000 per year. After a couple of years it increases to almost $100,000. So while the program is not cheap, it is like an investment in your long-term financial stability.



Objection: I don’t know if I can devote this much time. What if I fall behind? What if it’s too hard for me?


 What you say: Yes, the program is somewhat intense. That’s why we call it a bootcamp. But it is also an advantage. Since you can complete all the work in 6 months, you can find a job and change your career path that much faster. And we won’t let you fall behind. We have daily check-ins with all students to make sure and catch if someone is struggling. If that happens, we have tutors that will help you get back on track. For example, I’ve spoken to our tutors and they’ve often said “I can tell when a student is just nodding their head in agreement, but doesn’t actually understand my explanation”. Once they know their explanation is not being understood, they try a different tactic and a different way to explain the same concept. So it maybe harder for some people and easier for others, but ultimately, everyone who wants to, graduates. The main thing to remember is that you will never be left alone, one-on-one with your problem. There is always a member of our academic staff to help you get unstuck.



Objection: I’m still considering my options and looking at other programs.


 What you say: Of course. This is a big decision and you should weigh your options carefully. I would say that when comparing our bootcamp to other programs, it’s important to compare what type of job you can expect to get after you graduate. Our Web Development course, as I mentioned, prepares you to be a Full Stack Developer. That means you qualify for both the front and back end jobs, as well as both. Whereas most bootcamps only teach front end. That’s the visual side of apps and websites and by themselves these sets of skills are less valued than if you can do both. Another thing to compare is job placement. At the end of our program we guarantee job placement with our three industry partners if you chose to take that route. And even if you’d like to work for another company, we provide extensive job support like mock interview training and help setting up your portfolio.



Question: You mentioned three companies at which a job is guaranteed. Which companies are these?

 What you say: These are companies that we have an exclusive contract with. They are always in need of quality entry-level engineers. They change from time to time, so I don’t have the current list right now. But once you get into month #6, you will work with your career counselor to see which one is the most appropriate for you. Generally, they are large global IT companies with thousands of employees



Question: Why do you want to check my credit score? Why are you asking for my banking information? I’m not comfortable with this.


Note: If the person thinks their credit score is above 550, then don’t go through the credit check. But if s/he is not sure and starts to fill out the application and gets anxious, s/he might ask these questions. So if they know for sure that it’s below 550 than insist on the credit check. If they refuse, then politely end the call. If they are not sure what their score is, then allow them to move forward in the process and we will conduct the check after Python.


 What you say: As I mentioned, the program doesn’t cost anything until you land a job. This means, in essence, that we take on the expense of the program upfront and trust that you will repay the costs once you have a job. But we also want to make sure that you are not already under the burden of extensive debt or are in bankruptcy. Checking the credit score allows us to quickly affirm that you are in good financial standing. I understand it’s somewhat intrusive. If you know your credit score is 550 or above, we don’t have to go through this check. I do have to say that ultimately, if after the 7 day trial you decide to join the program, this paperwork will be part of the contract you’d need to sign. But you don’t have to worry about it right now, if you are not comfortable.



Question: I’m looking for a career change. Is this program about that? Does this have to do with employment?


Note: Many people that show up to these counseling sessions are confused about who we are and what we do. They probably spent the last week looking at dozens of websites, making appointments and researching ways to change careers. They might not even remember which program you are calling from. Take the time explain it.


 What you say: Absolutely. Our bootcamp takes 6 months to prepare you for a career change. You learn all the necessary skills to become an engineer and get a job in the tech industry. And after you graduate, we guarantee job placement at a large, IT company.
